Career Vision & Purpose

What Kind of Work I Want to Do

I am drawn to work that keeps me outdoors and engaged with changing seasons while also allowing time in the shop maintaining and improving equipment. One of the reasons I am passionate about agriculture is the variety it offers — from working cattle and planting crops to putting up hay and maintaining machinery. Each season brings new challenges and opportunities to learn. I thrive in environments where I can apply a wide range of skills and continue developing new ones, and I take pride in being adaptable and capable across many types of work.

Role in the Family Operation

My role in the family operation is to contribute to its long-term sustainability by acquiring land of my own and integrating it into our shared farming system. While the current operation is not large enough to support all siblings independently, expanding through individually owned acreage allows us to continue farming together. By sharing equipment, labor, and responsibilities while maintaining separate income streams, we can reduce pressure on any one person and strengthen the overall operation.

5–10 Year Vision

In the next 5–10 years, I see myself owning land of my own and contributing to a sustainable, growth-focused family operation. I plan to continue helping expand our cow-calf herd to approximately 150 head while sharing responsibilities and improving efficiency. At the same time, I intend to grow Sandhills Biochar into a viable business that supports land stewardship, soil health, and diversified farm income.

Why Agriculture Matters to Me

This field matters to me on several levels. First, it is what I genuinely love to do. Agriculture gives me a sense of purpose in helping feed people and contributing to something larger than myself. There is pride in being part of the small population responsible for producing food and caring for the land.

Beyond that, agriculture provides an environment that fosters strong families and communities. Growing up on a farm created a bond within my family that many people do not fully understand. It taught us responsibility, resilience, and a strong work ethic — values that have made it easier for us to succeed in the workforce and in life. I want my future children to have the same opportunities and foundation that I was given.

What Motivates Me

What motivates me most is having a sense of purpose and responsibility in the work I do. Knowing that my efforts contribute to feeding people, caring for the land, and strengthening my family's operation gives meaning to long days and difficult challenges. I am also motivated by growth — both personal and professional — and by the opportunity to continually learn new skills and improve systems. Ultimately, I am driven by the desire to build something lasting that supports my family, serves my community, and provides opportunities for the next generation.

Why It Matters to the Future

This field matters to the future because fewer people are growing up connected to agriculture, and many consumers are increasingly disconnected from where their food comes from. Ensuring a stable and sustainable food system requires knowledgeable producers, responsible land stewardship, and public understanding of agriculture's role in everyday life. I want to help strengthen that connection by contributing to efficient production, caring for the land, and supporting the next generation of agricultural leaders.
